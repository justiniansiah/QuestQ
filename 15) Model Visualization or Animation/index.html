Model Visualization or Animation

This animation shows how our normal queuing systems operates.

Arriving customers join the back of the queue; The front most customer places an order and pays, the storeowner then prepares the food and serves them before moving on to take the next customer's order. This system follows a traditional First-Come-First-Serve principle.

This animation shows how our alternative queuing systems would operate.

Arriving customers join the back of the first(ordering) queue as per the traditional queue; The front most customer places an order and pays. But, they now proceed to join the second(waiting) queue, adjacent to the order queue and the store owner continues to take the next customer's order and payment. (FCFS)

Meanwhile, those in the waiting queue will be waitng as a second store owner prepare their food. The orders are processed in parallel and it due to the different times it takes to preparent different foods, the customers are served whenever their ordered food arrives. (If two or more customers order the same food, then it is FCFS based on who ordered first).
